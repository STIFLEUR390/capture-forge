## 2026-06-19 — PRD Creation (Fast Path)

### Decision: MIT License
- **Context:** The brainstorming session listed MIT vs GPLv3 as an open question.
- **Decision:** MIT license, consistent with product brief and Oxichrome dependency.
- **Rationale:** Most permissive license, aligns with Oxichrome ecosystem, maximum adoption potential.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Frontmatter, Section 1 (Core Tenets).

### Decision: Distribution Strategy
- **Context:** Whether to distribute via Chrome Web Store, GitHub only, or both.
- **Decision:** Both. GitHub for source code from day 1, Chrome Web Store from V0.1.
- **Rationale:** Broader reach, easier installation for non-technical users (Marie, Karim personas), store presence builds credibility.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 1 (Core Tenets), Section 11 (Browser Compatibility).

### Decision: No Fixed Calendar
- **Context:** The brainstorming roadmap shows Q3 2026 for V0.1.
- **Decision:** No fixed date. Development proceeds at sustainable pace.
- **Rationale:** Open-source project without funding pressure. Quality over timeline.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 15 (Phased Roadmap) — no dates on phases.

### Decision: AudienceLens in PRD as Vision Section
- **Context:** The AudienceLens architecture document is speculative/visionary.
- **Decision:** Include as Section 8 (Vision Architecture) in the PRD.
- **Rationale:** Guides today's architectural decisions (storage format, message protocol, modularity) without committing to implementation timeline.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 8.

### Decision: Rust-First with JS Shims
- **Context:** Certain Chrome APIs and libraries are not available in Rust/WASM.
- **Decision:** Core business logic in Rust. JS shims only where no Rust alternative exists (tabCapture, FFmpeg, MediaPipe).
- **Rationale:** Maximizes Rust coverage while being pragmatic about ecosystem gaps.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 4 (Product Architecture → Rust → JS Strategy).

### Decision: Three Independent Sub-Products
- **Context:** Original brainstorming proposed monolithic approach.
- **Decision:** Recorder Core (P0), Editor + Overlay (P1), AI/Enrichment (P2) as independently shippable products.
- **Rationale:** A bug in AI never blocks recording. Parallel development. Gradual delivery.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 4 (Product Architecture), Sections 5-7.

### Decision: Single Mixed Track (MVP)
- **Context:** Whether to support multiple audio/video tracks in V0.1.
- **Decision:** Single mixed track (video + audio combined) in MVP. Multi-track in P1+.
- **Rationale:** Reduces complexity of chunk management, OPFS layout, and export pipeline.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 6.6 (Storage Layout), Section 14 (Non-Goals).

## 2026-06-19 — PRD Review & Rescope (Product Review Session)

### Decision: V0.1 Scoped to Recorder Reliability Core
- **Context:** The initial PRD had 13 user stories for V0.1, including storage manager UI, configurable keyboard shortcuts, quota display, and setup wizard.
- **Decision:** V0.1 reduces to 10 stories (REC-01 through REC-10). REC-11 (storage manager), REC-12 (configurable shortcuts), REC-13 (quota display) and REC-14 (IndexedDB fallback) deferred to V0.2/V0.3. Setup wizard deferred to V0.2.
- **Rationale:** V0.1's competitive advantage must be "reliable capture + crash recovery", not "feature-complete shell". Peripheral UX adds test surface without differentiating value at launch.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 6 (Sub-Product A), Section 18 (Phased Roadmap), Section 14 (Non-Goals).

### Decision: Quality Metrics Lead, Vanity Metrics Secondary
- **Context:** Original section 3 listed GitHub stars and CWS downloads as primary metrics.
- **Decision:** Reorder metrics so session completion rate, recovery success rate, start→first-frame latency, export success rate, and re-record frequency lead the section. Community metrics move to secondary.
- **Rationale:** Product quality metrics are actionable during development. Stars and downloads are lagging indicators that the team should monitor but not optimize for.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 3 (Success Metrics & Counter-Metrics).

### Decision: WebM in V0.1, MP4 in P1 (Explicit Contract)
- **Context:** The personas (especially Karim, QA) imply MP4 as a requirement, but V0.1 scope targets WebM.
- **Decision:** Add explicit "Format Strategy" subsection to Vision. V0.1 optimizes reliable capture; MP4 is P1 driven by Jira/Slack/Windows interop needs.
- **Rationale:** Prevents expectations mismatch. The PRD now clearly states the trade-off rather than leaving it implicit.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 1 (Vision → Format Strategy), Section 14 (Non-Goals).

### Decision: AudienceLens → Architectural North Star (Reduced Prominence)
- **Context:** The AudienceLens section was detailed enough that a reader could mistake it for a near-term roadmap item.
- **Decision:** Renumber to Section 9, add "Architectural North Star" header and a constraint-mapping table showing how it guides today's decisions. Remove "Built-In Lenses" detail depth; keep illustrative table.
- **Rationale:** Preserves the vision's architectural value without over-committing to timeline. The North Star framing signals "guidance, not roadmap."
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 9 (Architectural North Star: AudienceLens).

### Decision: Oxichrome Exit Strategy Section
- **Context:** Oxichrome v0.2 is a young dependency. Technical readers may be skeptical.
- **Decision:** Add explicit "Oxichrome Exit Strategy" subsection (5.3) covering: internal wrappers on browser APIs, framework-agnostic business modules, decoupled UI/runtime, and fork readiness.
- **Rationale:** Reduces perceived risk for technical evaluators. The existence of an exit strategy is more important than the specifics.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 5.3 (Oxichrome Exit Strategy).

### Decision: Add Product Principles Section
- **Context:** The PRD had implicit values (privacy, reliability) scattered across sections.
- **Decision:** Add a dedicated Product Principles section (Section 4) with 5 principles: local-first, no silent failure, standard export, optional intelligence, forkable architecture.
- **Rationale:** Gives the team a reference for design decisions without re-reading the full PRD.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 4 (Product Principles).

### Decision: Add Adoption Thesis Section
- **Context:** The PRD described who would use the product but not how it would reach them.
- **Decision:** Add Section 15 (Adoption Thesis) describing the beachhead (Dev/QA), growth mechanics (individual → recurring → intra-team → cross-role), and what GitHub stars actually measure vs what matters.
- **Rationale:** Aligns the team on growth model and prevents over-investment in vanity acquisition channels.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 15 (Adoption Thesis).

### Decision: Add Maintenance Model Section
- **Context:** No guidance existed for how contributions, breaking changes, and versioning would work.
- **Decision:** Add Section 16 (Maintenance Model) covering governance, contribution criteria, module compatibility, and stability policy per artifact type.
- **Rationale:** Essential for open-source projects to set expectations early. Prevents friction when the first external contributor arrives.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 16 (Maintenance Model).

### Decision: Rewrite Three Key Formulations
- **Context:** Three formulations were too absolute and could undermine credibility.
- **Decision:**
    - "most comprehensive open-source screen recorder" → "a privacy-first open-source screen recorder designed for resilient local capture"
    - "Zero limits" → "No artificial limits, within browser and device constraints"
    - "Available for Chrome V0.1 and Firefox V1.0" → "Chrome-first, with Firefox planned after the recorder core is stable"
- **Rationale:** Precision over hyperbole. The revised language is harder to fact-check negatively.
- **Recorded in PRD:** Section 1 (Vision).
